2020 Virtual Micromouse Competition

2020 Princeton Virtual Micromouse Competition

Public health measures led us to cancel our in-person competition scheduled for April, so we decided to make our competition virtual and hold it in the fall!

Registration Deadline: October 21, 2020

Algorithm submission deadline: November 20, 2020

Competition Overview

  • Each team submits a maze-solving algorithm, written in any of the allowable programming languages (currently Python 3 and C++).

  • There will be a time limit to solve the maze, where each movement or turn consumes a certain amount of time.

  • A team’s score is based on both its fastest start-to-finish run and its overall time consumption.

  • There will be a few competition mazes, and every team will be tasked to solve all the mazes.

  • Competition mazes will not be released until all teams submit their algorithms, but practice mazes will be made available.

  • Generally follows the rules of the IEEE’s Micromouse competition (though not an IEEE sponsored event), although many adaptations are made for the virtual format.

  • The maze consists of a 16x16 grid of tiles

  • The bottom-left tile is the start and the center 4 tiles are the finish.

  • Unlike our in-person micromouse competition, there will be no design presentation or design award.

  • Teams with the best scores will be streamed live on competition day. The date is TBD but will likely be a weekend in October or November.

  • Algorithm submission deadline: One week before competition day

Simulator

The simulator is now available as of July 31st.

A virtual machine for the simulator has been developed, and it can be run using VirtualBox. This is the easiest way to get started. The virtual machine is available here. The simulator can be launched with the desktop shortcut named “mms”.

If the simulator runs slowly on the virtual machine, participants can also install the simulator on their own machines. However, they must ensure their programs work on the virtual machine before submitting. To install the simulator, first clone or download this repository and then follow the instructions in the readme.



Sample Mazes

Three sample mazes are available here for download, as of October 21st. The competition mazes will resemble the characteristics of these sample mazes and will have a similar number of walls.
